Who is Jon Gunderson?

  • Background in engineering and human factors.
  • Learned about accessibility through working on building devices and writing software at the Trace Center at University of Wisconsin-Madison
  • 30 Years at the University of Illinois at Urbana/Champaign in Disability Services helping the university understand the accessibility barriers and solutions of the information age.
  • 25+ years in participating in the development of accessibility standards at the W3C:
    • User Agent Accessibility Guidelines 1.0
    • Accessible Rich Internet Applications (WAI-ARIA)
    • ARIA Authoring Practices
    • ARIA and Assistive Technologies Community Group
  • Developing open-source (e.g. free) tools to support accessibility:
    • AInspector for WCAG Evaluation
    • Headings, Landmarks and Links (H2L)
    • SkipTo.js
